Our Team

Little company, powerful code people.

Apsis started at brunch one morning in January, 2014. Noah and Wyatt — young and foolish — sat down to eat pancakes, and ended up quitting their jobs to start a company. The premise? "If we can't find enough work to survive, we're probably not very good developers."

11 years, 5 babies, & 4 weddings later things still haven't managed to fall apart. The rest, they say, is history.

Noah Callaway

Founding Partner

For over 10 years, Noah has been the technical backbone of the Apsis engineering team. In stark contrast to his cofounder, Noah’s history is about as textbook as it gets: CS classes at the University of Oregon while still in high school, BS from the University of Washington with specialization in physics and computer science, a stint at Microsoft, a tour at Amazon, and for the last decade plus, serving as Apsis’s lead engineer and architect. Noah’s drive for building the technically correct solution is balanced by his decade of experience running Apsis Labs, where his focus is on outcome-driven-development: finding the solution that delivers 90% of the value at 10% of the effort.

Outside of work, you’ll find Noah with his wife, dog, and two young children; or you’ll find him working, but this time on a new GUI layout engine in Rust or perhaps trying to get his video drivers to work correctly — he runs Linux after all.

    Wyatt Kirby

    Founding Partner

    An accidental developer, Wyatt came to software and small business by an unconventional route. As a cofounder at Apsis, he has helped build a team of exceptional engineers, one equipped to deliver effective solutions fast. After more than a decade, Wyatt keeps coming back to software because it offers a rare convergence of interests: writing, teaching, and tech. A graduate of Bennington College in Vermont, Wyatt’s appreciation for clear communication has come to define Apsis’s engineering team: understanding first, technology second. This focus leads to asking the right questions, finding the right answers, and delivering success year after year.

    After work, you’ll find Wyatt enjoying time with his wife and daughter, playing with the cat, roasting coffee, cooking dinner, or arguing on the internet about college football.

      Eric Greer

      Senior Engineer & Devops Specialist

      Eric is a full stack software developer with a passion for DevOps. Working with the nuances of AWS, he has worked move large clients and small into the cloud architecture that is right for them, automating deployments and ensuring applications stability. Eric has a B.S and M.S. in Computer Science from WPI, a background in IT support, combined with 15 years of software development projects which gives him an attention to detail and makes him a go to resource for any questions a client may have. Eric finds passion learning the nuances of each business and is always excited to tackle the next challenging project.

        Henry Keiter

        Senior Engineer & 1400 ELO

        Henry is an imported Coloradan who views software development as a window into everyone else’s fields. Every industry has fascinating hurdles that are completely unknown to the outside world, and software allows us to build a catapult to hurl ourselves over them, if we take the time to map the terrain first. Learning the landscape from domain experts is always a rich experience. Delivering a catapult that meets their actual needs is a fitting reward.

        Beyond the office, you’ll find Henry reading, gardening, or playing strategy games with family. Any physical catapults in the backyard are strictly coincidental.

          Chris Pfohl

          Senior Engineer & Data Engineer

          Chris is an expert data mover, munger, and analyzer. He builds robust third party integrations, answers tough questions with data, and brings order to databases and applications to empower new features and higher reliability.

              Joey Harwood
